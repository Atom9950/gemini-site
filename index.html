What Constitutes Ragging?

As per the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India & UGC Guidelines, ragging includes but is not limited to:

  • Any conduct by any student or students whether by words spoken or written or by an act which has the effect of teasing, treating or handling with rudeness a fresher or any other student.
  • Asking a student to do any act which such student will not in the ordinary course do, causing shame, torment, or embarrassment.
  • Any act of physical or mental abuse (including bullying and exclusion) targeted at another student on the ground of color, race, religion, caste, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, etc.

Consequences of Ragging

Students found guilty of ragging will face strict disciplinary action, which may include:

  • Immediate suspension from attending classes and academic privileges.
  • Withholding or withdrawing scholarships/fellowships.
  • Debarring from appearing in any test/examination or other evaluation process.
  • Suspension or expulsion from the hostel.
  • Rustication from the institution for a period ranging from 1 to 4 semesters.
  • Filing of a Police FIR (First Information Report) under the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
